For this video production project, I was asked to make a 60-75 second video showcasing something on Millersville's campus with a group of classmates. My group focused on the Millersville Orchard. We were required to make a storyboard, which is a basic outline of the clips that make the video. Below the video, you can find our storyboard. We also had to make a script of what we wanted to say in each clip. This can be found below the storyboards. Our first step in the production process was to make the storyboard. Next, we made a script to match each scene. Then, we went out and shot our clips. After we had all the clips, I edited them into a full video with music and transitions using Adobe Premiere Pro. I hope you enjoy the video!

Storyboards

Script

Scene 1: title, 10 sec Scene 2: describe FTPF and MU (and Lockhaven) partnership, 20 sec

Last fall, Millersville and two other universities partnered with The Fruit Tree Planting Foundation to create a fruit orchard on the Millersville and Lock Haven University campuses. The Fruit Tree Planting Foundation is an international charity with the goal of “planting fruit, nut, and medicinal trees to alleviate world hunger, combat global pollution of the environment, strengthen communities, and improve the surrounding air, soil, and water.” (https://www.ftpf.org/)

Scene 3: describe grant $ and information about partnership and teaching tools for BIOL, 10 sec

This partnership was made possible by a $7,988 Pennsylvania State System of Higher Education grant that was split between Lock Haven and Millersville University to fund all of the materials that were necessary to complete the orchard. In the future, the trees are going to be used as a teaching tool for Millersville’s Department of Biology.

Scene 4: describe fruit trees, 3-5 years to produce fruit, 20 seconds.

Last fall, the orchard was planted at Millersville University with the help of faculty and students, and it is being managed by Dr. Christopher Stieha. The orchard consists of a variety of 40 fruit trees, including apple, pear, plum, peach, and cherry. While they may not look like much now, these trees will continue to grow and are expected to produce fruit within three to five years.

Scene 5: credits, 5 sec (https://blogs.millersville.edu/news/2022/09/13/new-fruit-orchard-on-campus/) (https://www.abc27.com/local-news/fruit-orchard-coming-to-millersville-university/)
